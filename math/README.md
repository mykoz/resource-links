
####Gradient

For a function of two variables z=f(x,y), the gradient is the two-dimensional vector <f_x(x,y),f_y(x,y)>.  
(This definition generalizes in a natural way to functions of more than three variables.)  

For the function z = f(x,y)= 4x^2 + y^2  
grad(f) = df/dx (x,y,z) , df/dy (x,y,z) , df/dz (x,y,z)
grad(f) = 8x, 2y

Let f(x,y)=x2y. (a) Find ∇f(3,2). (b) Find the derivative of f in the direction of (1,2) at the point (3,2).
```math
Solution: (a) The gradient is just the vector of partial derivatives.  
The partial derivatives of f at the point (x,y)=(3,2) are:
∂f∂x(x,y)∂f∂x(3,2)=2xy=12∂f∂y(x,y)∂f∂y(3,2)=x2=9
Therefore, the gradient is
∇f(3,2)=12i+9j=(12,9).
```
